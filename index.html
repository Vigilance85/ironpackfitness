Here Are Straight Answers.

Do I need any special equipment to start? +
No. You need a backpack you can load with 15–20 lbs of weight (textbooks, a bag of rice in a sealed bag, or a cheap barbell plate wrapped in a towel all work). The guide covers gear selection at every budget level — from free improvisation to purpose-built rucksacks — in full detail.
I'm very overweight and out of shape. Is this too advanced for me? +
This program was specifically designed with you in mind. Phase I is three weeks of unweighted walking at your own pace. There is no load requirement until Week 4, and starting load is kept deliberately light. If you can walk to the end of your street, you can start this program.
How is the meal plan different from a standard diet? +
It's built around two distinct daily protocols: Ruck Day and Rest Day. Most diets treat every day identically, which is metabolically naive. On days you ruck, you need more carbohydrates to fuel the effort and support recovery. On rest days, you carb down and fat up. The result is a diet that works with your training instead of fighting it.
